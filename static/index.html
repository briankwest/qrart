        5 QR conditioning
        How hard the ControlNets pull the diffusion
      

      

        
        
        1.10
      

      
QR Monster conditioning weight. 1.05–1.20 for v1, 0.95–1.05 for v2. Above 1.30 the grid starts to dominate.


      

        
        
        1.00
      

      
How much of the canvas the QR occupies. 1.00 = fills frame. 0.70–0.80 leaves a gray margin where the prompt's scene grows around the QR — the community v2 trick.


      

        
        
        0.00
      

      
Stacks Tile ControlNet for photo coherence. 0 = off; 0.3–0.5 reduces QR-grid noise. ≥ 0.6 weakens the QR.


      

        
        
        0.30
      

      

        
        
        0.95
      

      
ControlNet active window (fraction of steps). Default 0.30 → 0.95: first 30% paints scene freely, QR Monster shapes 30–95%, last 5% finishes naturally. Use 0.00 → 1.00 for legacy full-window.

ADetailer (faces)

          
Detect faces with Haar cascade + re-render each at 512×512. Human faces only.

        

        

      

      

        

          
          
          0.35

        8 Run mode
        Speed, retry, and scan-gating behavior
      

      

        

          
Fast mode

          
LCM-LoRA + LCMScheduler. ~3× faster (6 steps, CFG ~1.5). Slight fidelity drop — great for iterating.

        

        

      

      

        

          
Auto-escalate

          
If 0 of N scan AND best score ≥ 0.70, retry as a follow-up job at scale +0.10 (capped 1.50). Requires Require-scan on.

        

        

      

      

        

          
Require scan

          
Gate "completed" on at least one candidate scanning. Off = ship even if no candidate decodes.
